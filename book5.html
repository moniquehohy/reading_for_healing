    • Book Blurb

      Black Beauty spends his youth in a loving home, surrounded by friends and cared for by his owners. But when circumstances change, he learns that not all humans are so kind. Passed from hand to hand, Black Beauty witnesses love and cruelty, wealth and poverty, friendship and hardship . . . Will the handsome horse ever find a happy and lasting home?

      Anna Sewell
    Editors Review

    Black Beauty is a renowned classic with many levels making it as enjoyable read for me now as it was as a bedtime story when I was a child. Beautifully written Black Beauty contains some rather grown up truths about the real world and the abuse animals can go through, as well as exploring the ideas that they can love and feel just as much as we can. For me, this was fascinating and really taught me the principles of respecting all creatures. To this day I still cry at many scenes throughout the book (and movie) but for me that’s one of the wonderful things about it – it can take me back to when pain is pain and happiness is happiness without all of the history or complications of real life.

    Amelia Seymour 2020
